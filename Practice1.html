                                                               Dalton's Atomic Theory

Although the origi of the idea that matter is composed od small indivisible particles called 'atomio' (meanning indivisible),
In 1808, a chemist named   John Dalton   published 'A New System of Chemical Philosophy', in which he proposed the following Postulates:

Postulates of Dalton's atomic theory-

  1. All matter consists of indivisible particles called atoms.
  2. All atoms of a given element have identical properties, including identical mass.
  3. Compounds are formed when atoms of different elements combine in a fixed ratio.
  4. Chemical reactions involve reorganisation of atoms. Therefore, atoms are neither created nor destroyed in a Chemical reaction but can be converted into another forms.

Drawbcaks of Dalton's Atomic Theory-

  • He could not explain the laws of chemical combination.
  • He could not explain the laws of gaseous volumes.
  • He counld not provide the reason for combining of atoms, which was answered later by other scientist.
